Twelve Pillars · Chapters 4-7 · Reflection

About "Twelve Pillars"

by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener

The story is about Michael Jones, a man who feels hopeless, and Charlie, his mentor, who teaches him the twelve keys to success. Mr. Davis, who is rich, gave Charlie these ideas. Every chapter reveals a new base that helps Michael make changes in his life.

Chapter Summaries

Chapter 4: Achieve Your Goals

Summary: The most important thing to remember from this chapter is to write down your goals. Charlie tells Michael, "The main reason to set a goal is for what it makes you when you reach it." Putting your goals down on paper makes them real and not just ideas in your head. This method also comes up with helpful ideas for how to actually reach those goals.

How I can apply this: I will start writing down my short-term and long term goals both academic and personal. Even achieving small goals will motivate me to aim higher in my computer science studies.

Chapter 5: The Proper Use of Time

Summary: This chapter shows that time is our most valuable resource because it is the only thing we can't get back. Michael learns from Charlie that there are many chances every day, but there is always one that is the best, and successful people focus on that one. The chapter talks about how important it is to manage our time well if we want to be successful and happy in life. Charlie tells Michael to stop wasting time and focus on what's important.

How I can apply this:I need to learn how to manage my time better as a computer science student with homework and projects. Each day, I need to focus on the most important things. I will find the best time for me to study deeply and make sure that nothing distracts me from it, like social media, games, music, or friends.

Chapter 6: Surround Yourself with the Best People

Summary:This chapter talks about how much the people around us affect our lives. Charlie's main piece of advice is, "Don't join a group that is too easy; you won't grow." Go where the expectations and the pressure to do well are high. The main point is that the people we spend time with can either help us or hurt us. Jim Rohn said that we are the average of the five people we spend the most time with. So, in order to be successful, we need to be purposeful about making friends, mentors, and peers who push us, share our values, and encourage us to improve. A network that is both supportive and ambitious gives you support, holds you accountable, and opens up new doors.

How I can apply this: I'll start paying closer attention to the people I surround myself with. I want to spend more time with classmates who actually care about their studies and have real goals they're working toward. These are people who can point me in the right direction as I work toward becoming a software developer.

Chapter 7: Be a Lifelong Learner

Summary:This chapter teaches one of the most important lessons in the book. Charlie tells Michael, "Formal education will help you make money; self-education will help you make a lot of money." The chapter makes it clear that you should keep learning even after you leave school. You need to be willing to learn on your own and keep learning throughout your life if you want to be very successful. You can do this by reading books, listening to audio programs, going to seminars, and looking for mentors. This constant search for knowledge broadens your view, improves your skills, and gives you access to new opportunities that formal education can't give you.

How I can apply this:As I work through my computer science degree, I won't just stick to what's in my textbooks. I want to make it a habit to read tech blogs, watch tutorials on new programming languages, and actually build my own projects to put what I'm learning into practice.
